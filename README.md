# Transferable Gas Production Forecasting Using Multi-Well Opera-tional Data: A Machine-Learning Based Approach

Gas production is a critical aspect of reservoir management, as natural gas is often produced alongside crude oil or as the primary hydrocarbon in gas fields. Accurate gas production fore-casting is critical for optimizing field operations and managing reservoir performance. Recent studies have implemented machine learning (ML) to forecast gas production accurately. However, previous ML models that have been trained based solely on lagged gas production data often fail to capture the underlying reservoir physics, making them difficult to generalize across various fields. In other cases, previous ML models have also been developed for single-well forecasting, thereby lacking the generalizability necessary for multi-field applications. In this study, we propose a physics-guided data-driven modeling approach that integrates key operational parameters, such as average downhole pressure, average wellhead pressure, average wellhead temperature, and average choke size percentage, collected from multiple wells to predict gas production. By combining these physically meaningful inputs with ML techniques such as Random Forest (RF), and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) Neural Networks, our model is designed to capture both the dynamic behavior of gas flow and the underlying reservoir charac-teristics across diverse operational environments. A comprehensive dataset was assembled from 5 production wells in the Volve field, with each well contributing measurements of the five operational variables. The results showed that LSTM had R2 score and MAE of 0.913 and 16,176.521m3, respectively, while RF had 0.828 and 27,227.740m3, respectively. The LSTM achieved a 10.27% higher R² score than the RF model, indicating significantly better explanatory power for gas production variance. In addition, the LSTM reduced the MAE by 40.60% compared to RF, demonstrating substantially lower prediction errors. The proposed deep neural network can provide robust and generalizable gas production forecasts, thereby offering a valuable tool for decision support in oil and gas operations.
